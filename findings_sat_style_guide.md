# Digital SAT Question Style Guide & Difficulty Calibration

This guide establishes the rules and templates for generating high-quality, non-copyrighted questions for AuraPrep that perfectly match the official College Board SAT style, tone, and difficulty calibration.

---

## ⚖️ Part 1: Calibrating Difficulty (Avoiding Misconceptions)

A common mistake in test prep is making "Easy" questions too trivial (e.g., $x + 2 = 5$) and "Hard" questions too complex or out-of-scope (e.g., complex calculus or trigonometry proofs). AuraPrep questions must align with the exact bands of the official Digital SAT:

### 1. Math Difficulty Bands

| Tier | Characteristics | Typical Topic Example | Desmos Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Easy** | 1-step problems, direct retrieval, basic conceptual tests. | Find the y-intercept of $f(x) = -4x + 12$. | Can be used, but solving by hand is faster. |
| **Medium** | 2-step problems, standard system of equations, linear models, basic quadratics. | Solve a system like: <br>$3x - y = 7$<br>$2x + 3y = 12$ | Extremely high. Easily cheesed by graphing intersections. |
| **Hard** | Multi-concept, complex algebraic manipulation, systems of inequalities, advanced circle equations, standard deviation comparisons, quadratic constant systems. | Find the constant $k$ in $y = x^2 + kx + 16$ if the equation has exactly one x-intercept. | Essential tool for visualization, but requires abstract set-up first. |

### 2. Reading & Writing Difficulty Bands

| Tier | Characteristics | Typical Topic Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Easy** | Straightforward grammar conventions, simple verb tense, obvious vocabulary context clues. | Single subject-verb agreement (e.g., "The group [of students] **is** going..."). |
| **Medium** | Advanced punctuation boundaries, modifier placement, standard transition logic (contrast/addition). | Connecting an independent clause and dependent clause using appropriate punctuation (semicolon vs. comma). |
| **Hard** | Complex rhetorical synthesis from notes with highly specific prompts, quantitative command of evidence (interpreting complex tables/graphs), logical inferences from dense academic prose. | Analyzing bulleted research notes about a specific scientific study and selecting the choice that matches a very narrow prompt condition. |

## 📝 Part 2: Non-Copyrighted Phrasing Templates (Easy, Medium, Hard)

Use these templates to generate new questions. Modify the scenarios, numbers, and subjects to keep them unique.

### 1. Math Section Templates

#### **A. Easy Math Template (Algebra: Linear Functions)**
* **Template Concept:** Direct linear word problem evaluating a starting value or rate.
* **Template text:**
  > A local landscaping service charges a flat fee of $\$A$ plus $\$B$ per hour for lawn maintenance. The total charge, $C(h)$, in dollars, for $h$ hours of maintenance is given by the function $C(h) = B h + A$. What is the charge, in dollars, for a job that takes $T$ hours?
  >
  > A) $(B \cdot T) + A$ *[Correct]*
  > B) $(A \cdot T) + B$
  > C) $(A + B) \cdot T$
  > D) $A \cdot B \cdot T$
* **How to vary:** Change "landscaping service" to "bike rental company", "freelance programmer", or "hauling company". Modify the constants $A$, $B$, and $T$.

#### **B. Medium Math Template (Coordinate Geometry: Circles)**
* **Template Concept:** Identifying circle properties from an expanded equation.
* **Template text:**
  > In the xy-plane, the equation of a circle is given by $x^2 + y^2 - Ax + By + C = 0$. What is the radius of the circle?
  >
  > *[Example values: $x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 8y - 11 = 0$]*
  >
  > A) $3$
  > B) $4$
  > C) $6$ *[Correct, since radius $r = \sqrt{3^2 + (-4)^2 - (-11)} = \sqrt{36} = 6$]*
  > D) $36$
* **How to vary:** Modify the coefficients of the linear terms ($A$ and $B$) and the constant $C$ to yield integer radius values.

#### **C. Hard Math Template (Advanced Math: Quadratic Constants)**
* **Template Concept:** Unknown constant in a quadratic system with specific solutions.
* **Template text:**
  > In the system of equations below, $k$ is a constant. If the system has exactly one real solution, what is the value of $k$?
  >
  > $y = -2x^2 + kx - 8$
  > $y = Ax + B$
  >
  > A) [Value 1]
  > B) [Value 2] *[Correct, solved by setting equations equal, finding discriminant $b^2 - 4ac = 0$]*
  > C) [Value 3]
  > D) [Value 4]

### 2. Reading & Writing Section Templates

#### **A. Easy R/W Template (Standard English Conventions: Verb Tenses)**
* **Template Concept:** Identifying verb tense based on explicit time marker.
* **Template text:**
  > In [Year], marine biologist [Name] ________ a study on the migration patterns of humpback whales in the Pacific Ocean. The findings, published later that decade, established a clear link between water temperature shifts and feeding routes.
  >
  > A) initiates
  > B) initiated *[Correct - past tense indicated by historical year]*
  > C) will initiate
  > D) is initiating
* **How to vary:** Change the year, the name of the scientist, and the topic of study (e.g., "In 1984, botanist Elena Rostova began...").

#### **B. Medium R/W Template (Standard English Conventions: Punctuation Boundaries)**
* **Template Concept:** Testing colons vs. semicolons with independent and dependent clauses.
* **Template text:**
  > Many agricultural experts advocate for crop rotation; ________ this practice not only replenishes soil nutrients but also disrupts pest cycles.
  >
  > A) rotation;
  > B) rotation: *[Correct - colons introduce explanations preceded by an independent clause]*
  > C) rotation,
  > D) rotation

#### **C. Hard R/W Template (Expression of Ideas: Rhetorical Synthesis)**
* **Template Concept:** Selecting an answer that meets a highly specific goal from a set of bulleted research notes.
* **Template text:**
  > While researching a topic, a student has taken the following notes:
  > * Fictional element X was discovered in year Y by team Z.
  > * It exhibits high thermal conductivity.
  > * It is extremely rare, composing only 0.001% of the crust.
  > * Fictional element W was discovered in year V by team U.
  > * It has low thermal conductivity but high electrical conductivity.
  >
  > The student wants to contrast the thermal conductivities of element X and element W. Which choice most effectively uses information from the notes to accomplish this goal?
  >
  > A) Element X has high thermal conductivity, whereas element W has low thermal conductivity. *[Correct]*
  > B) Element X and element W were discovered by different research teams.
  > C) Both elements exhibit conductive properties.
  > D) Discovered in Y, element X is rare and has high thermal conductivity.
* **How to vary:** Create new fictional elements/compounds or scientific research objects. Ensure the distractors are factually true according to the bullets but fail to address the specific "contrast the thermal conductivities" prompt.
